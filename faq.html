Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about learning to fly at Chicago Midway with Aviation Professionals Inc.

Request informationCall 773 284-5678
How do I get started?
The first step is a phone call. Tell us what you want to fly, whether that's flying for fun or a career, and we'll lay out exactly how to get there. You can request information or call 773 284‑5678.
Do I need a medical certificate?
For a Private Pilot Certificate you'll need at least an FAA third-class medical certificate, issued by an Aviation Medical Examiner. We can point you to local examiners and help you time it correctly in your training.
How long does it take to earn a Private Pilot Certificate?
It depends mostly on how often you fly. The FAA minimum is 40 flight hours; most students finish in roughly 60-70. Flying consistently, once or twice a week, is the single biggest factor in finishing efficiently.
What aircraft will I train in?
Single-engine training airplanes based on the ramp at Chicago Midway, the same environment the airlines use.
Where are you located?
Chicago Midway International Airport (MDW), on the southwest side of Chicago. We've operated from Midway since 1975.
Do you offer ground school?
Yes. Ground instruction is built around Attitude Flying and paced to you alongside your flight lessons.
Do I have to sign a contract?
No. There are no contracts, no membership, and no dues, you pay as you fly, and we're open seven days a week, so you train at the pace that fits your life.
What is Attitude Flying?
It's API's copyrighted, horizon-first teaching method: you learn to control the airplane by the visual picture out the window before chasing instruments. Read more about the method.
Can I train while working or on my own schedule?
Absolutely. Because you pay as you fly and we're open every day, students fit training around work and family on whatever schedule works for them.
